**Reasoning and suggested adjustments**

The current performance ( f1 ≈ 0.99 ) shows that the Random Forest separates the masquerade‑fault traffic from normal traffic very well. The SHAP analysis tells us that the most important features are the various *TrapAreaSum* values, the analog delta and the `cbStatus` flag. To make the attack harder to detect we should:

1. **Lower the fault occurrence probability** – a slightly smaller `fault.prob` reduces the overall frequency of the fault pattern, making it blend more with normal traffic while still keeping the attack realistic (the guideline forbids values that are “very low”).  
2. **Reduce the magnitude of the analog deviation** – decreasing `analog.deltaAbs.max` makes the analog signal change less conspicuous, shrinking the gap between attack and normal analog values.  
3. **Make the trap‑area spikes rarer** – lowering `trapArea.spikeProb` reduces the number of pronounced spikes that the model currently uses as a strong discriminator.  
4. **Narrow the trap‑area multiplier range** – moving both the minimum and maximum of `trapArea.multiplier` closer to 1 (e.g., 0.99 – 1.05) makes the overall trap‑area scaling less extreme, further blurring the distinction.

All these changes are gradual, stay within the allowed ranges, and respect the “no degenerate variant” constraints.
