**Reasoning and suggested adjustments**

The current Random Forest model relies heavily on the *TrapArea*‑related sums, the analog delta values and the `cbStatus` flag. To make the masquerade‑fault traffic blend more with normal traffic we should:

1. **Lower the distinctiveness of the trap‑area spikes** – bring the multiplier range closer to 1 and reduce the spike probability. This will shrink the amplitude of the trap‑area feature values that the model currently uses as strong discriminators.

2. **Narrow the analog variation** – decreasing the maximum absolute analog delta makes the analog feature distribution overlap more with normal samples, without eliminating the variation completely (the minimum stays at 0.1).

3. **Increase the overall fault occurrence probability** – a slightly higher `fault.prob` makes attacks appear more frequently, which can reduce the model’s ability to treat them as rare outliers.

4. **Slightly extend the fault duration range** – a larger `fault.durationMs.max` adds variability to the temporal pattern, making it harder for the classifier to learn a tight time‑window signature.

5. **Reduce the TTL (time‑to‑live) of GOOSE messages** – a smaller `ttlMsValues` shortens the lifespan of each message, causing more inter‑mixing of attack and normal packets in the sliding‑window features.

All these changes stay within the allowed ranges, keep the attack recognizable as a masquerade fault, and avoid the degenerate configurations that were explicitly prohibited.
