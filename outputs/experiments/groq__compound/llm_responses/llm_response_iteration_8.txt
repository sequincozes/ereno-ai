**Rationale for the suggested changes**

The current Random Forest model relies heavily on the features **TrapAreaSum**, the analog delta values, and **cbStatus**. To make the masquerade‑fault attack harder to separate from normal traffic we should:

1. **Lower the fault probability modestly** – decreasing `fault.prob` reduces how often the fault pattern appears, making it less dominant in the training data while still keeping it realistic (the constraint is not to go too low).  
2. **Bring the analog delta range closer to normal values** – shrinking both the minimum and maximum of `analog.deltaAbs` narrows the distinction between attack‑related analog variations and benign fluctuations.  
3. **Widen the trap‑area multiplier range** – expanding the interval makes the trap‑area values more variable, overlapping more with normal traffic and thus reducing the discriminative power of the `TrapAreaSum`‑related features.  
4. **Keep `trapArea.spikeProb` unchanged** – the guidelines forbid setting it to zero, and keeping it at its current level preserves plausibility while the other adjustments already introduce enough ambiguity.

All modifications stay within the allowed fields, respect the “no‑degenerate” constraints, and are incremental enough to remain realistic for a synthetic GOOSE dataset.
