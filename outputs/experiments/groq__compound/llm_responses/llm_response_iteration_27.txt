**Rationale for the suggested changes**

The current Random‑Forest model relies heavily on the *TrapArea*‑related sums, the analog delta values and the `cbStatus` flag. To make the masquerade‑fault traffic blend more with normal GOOSE traffic we should:

1. **Lower the magnitude of the analog deviation** – decreasing `analog.deltaAbs.max` brings the analog values of the attack closer to the normal range, reducing the importance of the “analog delta” feature.
2. **Bring the trap‑area multiplier nearer to 1** – shrinking `trapArea.multiplier.max` (and optionally the minimum) reduces the size of the “TrapAreaSum” spikes that the model currently uses as a strong discriminator.
3. **Increase the spike probability slightly** – a modest rise in `trapArea.spikeProb` makes spikes occur more often, turning them into a less distinctive pattern.
4. **Adjust the fault probability** – moving `fault.prob` a little upward (from 0.35 to 0.4) raises the overall prevalence of the fault, making the attack less “rare” and therefore harder for the classifier to separate from normal traffic.
5. **Widen the fault duration window** – expanding the range (`fault.durationMs.min` and `fault.durationMs.max`) adds variability to the temporal profile of the attack, which can further blur the distinction.

All these modifications stay within the allowed ranges, avoid degenerate configurations (no zeroing of key parameters), and are incremental enough to remain realistic for a synthetic GOOSE dataset.
