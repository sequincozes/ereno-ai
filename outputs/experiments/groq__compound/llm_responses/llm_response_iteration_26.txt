**Rationale**

The Random Forest model relies heavily on the *TrapArea*‑related sums, the analog delta values and the `cbStatus` flag. To make the masquerade‑fault traffic blend more with normal GOOSE traffic we should:

1. **Narrow the amplitude of the analog disturbance** – a smaller `analog.deltaAbs` range makes the voltage/current variations look less anomalous.
2. **Bring the trap‑area scaling closer to the nominal value** – moving the multiplier interval toward 1.0 reduces the spike in the `TrapAreaSum` features that the model currently uses.
3. **Slightly lower the probability of a trap‑area spike** – a lower `trapArea.spikeProb` means fewer conspicuous spikes.
4. **Make the fault duration a bit longer and less deterministic** – widening the duration window (and raising the minimum) spreads the fault over more samples, reducing the sharpness of the temporal pattern.
5. **Increase the overall fault occurrence probability modestly** – a slightly higher `fault.prob` adds more attack instances, which can dilute the distinction between attack and normal samples without making the attack unrealistic.

All these adjustments stay within the allowed ranges, avoid degenerate configurations, and are incremental enough to keep the synthetic scenario plausible while reducing the separability that the IDS currently exploits.
